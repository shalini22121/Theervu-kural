History and Geography

Kanyakumari, located at the southernmost tip of India, is known for its historical and cultural significance. It was ruled by the Chera, Chola, and Pandya dynasties and later became part of the Travancore kingdom. The town is unique as it is the meeting point of the Indian Ocean, the Bay of Bengal, and the Arabian Sea. Kanyakumari is famous for its stunning sunrise and sunset views over the ocean. The region has a tropical climate with moderate temperatures throughout the year.

Tourism

Kanyakumari is a popular tourist destination with several iconic attractions. The Vivekananda Rock Memorial, located on a small island, honors the great spiritual leader Swami Vivekananda. The Thiruvalluvar Statue, a 133-foot tall statue of the famous Tamil poet, stands beside the memorial. The Kanyakumari Temple, dedicated to Goddess Kanyakumari, is a revered pilgrimage site. Sunset and sunrise views from the Kanyakumari beach attract visitors from all over the world. Padmanabhapuram Palace, an ancient wooden palace of the Travancore kings, showcases Kerala-style architecture.
