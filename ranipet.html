History and Geography

Ranipet, a district in Tamil Nadu, is known for its industrial and historical significance. The region was historically part of the Arcot Nawabs' rule and later came under British control. It is located on the banks of the Palar River and has a hot and dry climate for most of the year. The district is a major hub for leather industries and is one of the largest exporters of leather goods in India. Agriculture is also practiced in some parts, with crops like paddy and sugarcane being cultivated.

Tourism

Ranipet has several places of interest for visitors. The Jalakandeswarar Temple in nearby Vellore is an ancient temple known for its Dravidian architecture. The Vellore Fort, a historical fort built during the Vijayanagara Empire, is a major tourist attraction. The Amirthi Zoological Park, located near Ranipet, is a peaceful spot for nature lovers and families. The Golden Temple in Sripuram, a spiritual destination with a magnificent gold-plated structure, attracts thousands of devotees. The Palar River, which flows through the district, offers scenic views and is an important water source for the region.
