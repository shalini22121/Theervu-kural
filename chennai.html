History and Geography

Chennai, formerly known as Madras, is the capital city of Tamil Nadu, India. Its history dates back over 2,000 years, with the first historical mention of the area in the ancient Tamil literature. The city was originally a small fishing village called "Madrasi" in the 1st century CE, but it grew significantly in the 17th century when the British East India Company established Fort St. George in 1644. This fort became the foundation for the modern city of Chennai. The British expanded the city, and it became an important port and trading center during the colonial period. After India gained independence in 1947, the city was renamed Chennai in 1996, reflecting its Tamil heritage.

Tourism

Kallakurichi, a district in Tamil Nadu, was formed in 2019, separating from Villupuram. Historically influenced by major empires, it later fell under British rule. Agriculture is vital, with crops like paddy and sugarcane thriving. Kallakurichi is now developing as an education and industry hub. This contributes to Tamil Nadu's overall progress.
